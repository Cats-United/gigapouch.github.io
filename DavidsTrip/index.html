David's trip
I live in Campbell. We traveled to Crater Lake which was about an 8 hour drive and stayed there for two nights. Here are some pictures of the beautifully blue lake:
Crater Lake was formed by a volcano that erupted and collapsed on itself. Years of snowmelt filled the area in between the rim and the caldera, forming the lake and Wizard Island:
We saw a lot of ground squirrels there. Here's one of them:
Later, we drove around the lake on Rim Drive, a 30 mile road with many scenic overlooks. Here are the beautiful pictures we took from it:
The next morning, before going off to see relatives in Lebanon, OR, we stopped along the way for a short 1 mile hike to see a cool waterfall and feel the mist spraying and water splashing into the creek.
There is no word to describe how many lakes, trees, and rivers there are in Oregon. We stayed with our relatives for 3 nights and did lots of fun stuff. Then we all drove up together to Seattle to go to the Museum of Flight, then go to the Boeing factory for a tour the next morning. Here are some of the planes we saw:
Then 2 days later, we went hiking in the Tillamook National Forest (not listed on map) and saw yet another creek and waterfall:
Then after another night at my relatives' house, we started driving back home. We stopped for lunch after about 90 miles of driving, in the little town of Oakland, Oregon.
Finally, 220 miles later, we stopped for the night in Dunsmuir, in the Railroad Park Resort, because that is pretty much half way between Lebanon and Campbell. You get to sleep in a caboose that is almost like a hotel room on the inside.
And that sums up my trip!
